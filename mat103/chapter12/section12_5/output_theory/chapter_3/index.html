EXAMPLE 3 Show that the lines \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) with parametric equations \[ L_1: x = 1 + t \quad y = -2 + 3t \quad z = 4 - t \] \[ L_2: x = 2s \quad y = 3 + s \quad z = -3 + 4s \] are skew lines; that is, they do not intersect and are not parallel (and therefore do not lie in the same plane).

SOLUTION The lines are not parallel because the corresponding direction vectors \(\langle 1, 3, -1 \rangle\) and \(\langle 2, 1, 4 \rangle\) are not parallel. (Their components are not proportional.) If \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) had a point of intersection, there would be values of \(t\) and \(s\) such that \[ 1 + t = 2s \] \[ -2 + 3t = 3 + s \] \[ 4 - t = -3 + 4s \] But if we solve the first two equations, we get \(t = \frac{11}{5}\) and \(s = \frac{8}{5}\), and these values donâ€™t satisfy the third equation. Therefore there are no values of \(t\) and \(s\) that satisfy the three equations, so \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) do not intersect. Thus \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) are skew lines.
